DATA SOURCES AND METHODOLOGY FOR MAMMALIAN BRAIN MASS DATASET

Primary Data Sources:
1. Dryad Dataset (Source 1)
   - URL: https://datadryad.org/dataset/doi:10.5061/dryad.tr7m5
   - Contains: 85 exact species measurements
   - Includes: Primates, rodents, carnivores, ungulates
   - Example species: Baboon, Chimpanzee, Cat, Zebra

2. Estimated Values (Source 2)
   - Based on phylogenetic relatives from:
     a) Gittleman (1986) - Carnivore brain sizes
       DOI: 10.1111/j.1095-8312.1986.tb01773.x
     b) Marino (1998) - Cetacean neuroanatomy
       DOI: 10.1002/(SICI)1096-9861(19980420)393:4<505::AID-CNE8>3.0.CO;2-9
     c) Isler et al. (2008) - Primate/marsupial scaling
       DOI: 10.1111/j.1558-5646.2008.00330.x
     d) Kruska (2005) - Ungulate brain evolution
       DOI: 10.1007/s00359-005-0060-y

Methodology:
1. For species in Dryad:
   - Used exact values when available
   - Consolidated numbered variants (e.g., Cat1-Cat6 → Cat)

2. For missing species:
   - Identified closest taxonomic relative
   - Scaled brain mass using allometric equations:
     EQ = Brain Mass / (0.12 × Body Mass^0.67)
   - Verified against published literature

3. Special cases:
   - Bats: Used Sarko et al. (2009) as reference
     DOI: 10.1002/cne.22101
   - Marine mammals: Used Marino (1998) studies
   - Rodents: Used Manger et al. (2013)
     DOI: 10.1002/cne.23364

Complete Dataset References:

Quality Control:
- All estimated values flagged as Source 2
- Taxonomic verification using IUCN/Smithsonian databases
- Cross-checked against 10+ published allometry studies
